Scale-up efforts to prevent, detect and control hypertension: WHO



  • Hypertension is the single most important leading cause of cardiovascular disease mortality globally and in the WHO South-East Asia Region. With almost half of the people with hypertension in the Region unaware of their condition there is urgent need to scale up hypertension services, to prevent, detect and control hypertension.

Heroes of Public Health awards



  • WHO conferred ‘Heroes of Public Health’ awards to Dr G Nageswara Rao from India, Dr. Suwit Wibulpolprasert from Thailand, and Dr Virna Maria Gusmao dos Reis Martins-Sam from Timor-Leste on the occasion of the World Health Day. The awards were given by Regional Director Dr Poonam Khetrapal Singh at an event in New Delhi. Dr G Nageswara Rao, founder-Chair of L V Prasad Eye Institute (LVPEI), was awarded for the tireless and generous efforts of the LVPEI which has positively impacted lives of more than 30.64 million people.

Urgently address gaps in leprosy services and accelerate efforts towards zero leprosy infection and disease: WHO



  • The World Health Organization today called on countries in the South-East Asia Region and globally to urgently address gaps in leprosy services disrupted by the COVID-19 pandemic and to accelerate efforts towards zero leprosy infection and disease, zero leprosy disability, and zero leprosy stigma and discrimination -the vision of the WHO Global Leprosy Strategy 2021-2030.
